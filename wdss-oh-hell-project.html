Project Overview

This project will oversee the deployment of a Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent with the aim of gaining strategical insight into the game, Oh Hell. Oh Hell is a classic trick-taking card game first documented around 1930. It has been described as “one of the best round games” and was an all-time favourite of former US president Bill Clinton; it was taught to him by Steven Spielberg. The dealer deals a set number of cards to each player including himself and places a card face up in the centre (trump card). The player to the left of the dealer (small blind) initiates the bidding. The aim is to correctly guess the number of tricks (wins) you will take that round, and the total number of tricks must not equal to the number of cards dealt to each player in the round. During play, the small blind leads with the first trick (leading card), and players must follow the same suit unless unable to; otherwise, they may discard or trump. The highest trump wins the round, but if no trumps are played then the highest card of the leading suit wins. The trick winner begins the next trick. Points are awarded to those who correctly guessed the number of tricks they would take that round; incorrect guesses result in a negative point. A common variant of the game, Up and Down the River, sees the number of cards dealt start at 1, increase to 10 before coming back to 1 for a total of 19 rounds played. We will aim to gain tactical insight into this version of the game by building an RL agent which will play against itself as well as other people.

Objectives

  • Build and train the RL agent within a local environment.
  • Identify any ‘tactics’ used by the agent following training.
  • Build an online interface that allows the human players to play against the agent.
