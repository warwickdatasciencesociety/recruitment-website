WDSS: Signal Processing Project

Signal Processing Project

  • WDSS
  • Coventry, UK (Remote)
  • Temporary

Take part in this guided Signal Processing project ran by WDSS, building essential team-working experience and supercharging your CV with real-world experience.

The final product will be published on WDSS's research blog.

Project Overview

  • Use independent component analysis to separate musical sources based on multiple recordings.
  • Setup a real life experiment to collect combined source data.
  • Evalutate different ICA methods and apply noise-reduction methods.
  • Explain basic signal processing techniques such as the Fourier Transform.

Role Responsibilities

(Audio/Electrical) Engineer
  • Setup a real-life experiment to collect combined source data.
  • Research and implement signal processing techniques.
  • Contribute to relevant sections of the final write-up.
Machine Learning Practitioner
  • Implement a variety of ICA algorithms and evaluate their performances.
  • Using statistical learning techniques to handle noisy inputs.
  • Contribute to relevant sections of the final write-up.

Candidate Attributes

  • A passion for the project and, preferably, interdisiplinary data science in general.
  • Self-starting attitude with willingness to research and problem-solve independently.
  • Ability to work well in a team and clearly communicate your ideas.
  • Strong time-management and organisational skills.
  • Expertise in data science or your domain is not required as this is a guided project.
